— CARTOGRAPHER STATEMENT —

Lan Samantha Chang's "Water Names" follows three Chinese American sisters listening to their grandmother Waipuo tell a story about an ancestor's daughter who was lured by the river and disappeared during a flood. The story explores oral tradition, desire, and the distance between generations in an immigrant family.

MAP ELEMENTS

The map is divided into four symbolic terrains. The Dried Lake is a cracked lakebed where layers of sediment represent layers of memory, corresponding to storytelling and cultural preservation, anchored by the quote "The red dot flickered and danced, making ghostly shapes as she moved her hands like a magician in the dark" (p. 116), which shows that Waipuo's storytelling is not casual but an act of keeping centuries alive. The Glowing Lake represents desire and temptation, a beautiful surface hiding danger. The quote "Or perhaps she lost her mind to desiring" (p. 119) is placed here because Chang deliberately leaves the daughter's fate ambiguous — the word "perhaps" refuses to judge whether desire is madness or courage. The Bridge Between Worlds represents the generational gap. The quote "We had only known blue swimming pools" (p. 117) captures the divide: the granddaughters' safe, chlorinated experience of water versus the wild Yangtze their family came from.

LEGEND SYMBOLS

The Red Dot (Waipuo's cigarette tip) marks places tied to storytelling — it appears at Waipuo's Stone Chair and The Unanswered Question because both involve the act of passing down or withholding narrative. The Pearl marks desire, appearing where characters are pulled toward something they cannot have, including Lily's Buried Cache, where old-world treasures that "would be only a bauble" (p. 118) in America echo the pearl ring's lost value. The Water Drop marks ancestral power, placed at The Broken Dam and The Mosquito Lanterns because even on an American porch, the family carries "the spirit of the river" (p. 116) inside them — water is not a place but an identity that travels across generations.
